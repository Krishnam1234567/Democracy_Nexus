Types of Elections in India

National

General Elections (Lok Sabha)

Held every 5 years to elect 543 Members of Parliament to the Lower House. The party/coalition with majority forms the central government.

  • Conducted by the Election Commission of India
  • Uses First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) system
  • Largest democratic exercise in the world
State

State Elections (Vidhan Sabha)

Held every 5 years in each state to elect members of the State Legislative Assembly. The winning party forms the state government.

  • Each state has its own election schedule
  • Number of seats varies by state population
  • Can be held simultaneously with Lok Sabha elections
Local

Local Body Elections

Elections to Panchayats (rural) and Municipalities (urban) for grassroots governance. Strengthened by the 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments.

  • Three-tier system: Village, Block, District
  • Mandatory reservation for SC/ST and women
  • Conducted by State Election Commissions
Special

Presidential & Other Elections

The President is elected by an Electoral College. Rajya Sabha elections use proportional representation with a single transferable vote system.

  • President elected by MPs and MLAs
  • Rajya Sabha: 1/3 members retire every 2 years
  • By-elections fill casual vacancies
